Precision Agriculture using IoT (IIIT-Hyderbad)

May 2021- July 2021

The growing population has led to a rise in food demand over the years, a recent article from Harvard Business Review has stated that the food demand is expected to increase anywhere between 59% to 98% by 2050. Precision agriculture will play a very important role in the upcoming years in meeting the increased demand. This project with the help of Smart sensors, IoT Infrastructure, and modern machine learning algorithms can help us cope up with this demand surge. Similar to how humans communicate using sound, plants communicate with each other by the release of VOCs (Volatile Organic Compounds). The new trailblazing sensor technology has made it possible for us to detect these VOCs released by the plant under different stress conditions (lack of water, light, High temperature). This data paired with Machine learning can be used to communicate with plants and understand their needs (water, light, space, CO2 ppm etc). Hence resulting in more efficient use of available resources and increased yield.
